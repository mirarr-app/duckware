Audit

From clipboard dump to portfolio frame

Screenshot.rocks is a browser-based mockup studio for people who ship UI and need it to look intentional outside the app. You feed it a screenshot; it returns that same surface nested inside browser chrome or a phone bezel, sitting on a colorful backdrop you can tune without opening Figma.

The project is MIT-licensed and self-hostable—clone, yarn install, yarn start—so teams can keep the generator on their own network. One-click extensions for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge collapse the loop further: capture a tab, land in the mockup editor.

01 · Friction

Capture → frame in one hop

Browser extensions skip the download-upload ritual. Designers and indie founders can go from live tab to shareable mockup while the idea is still warm.

02 · Craft

Browser and mobile frames

The output language matches how products are presented: traffic-light chrome, address bars, and phone shells that make a bare PNG read like a product still.

03 · Ownership

Open source, self-hostable

A full Yarn-based codebase means you can pin versions, fork frame styles, or run the studio offline when marketing assets cannot leave the building.

Workflow

How a mockup gets made

The mental model is a tiny design canvas—not a full DTP suite. Pick a frame, place the shot, paint the stage, export.

Verdict

A small studio with a loud output

Screenshot.rocks does one job with unusual clarity: make screenshots look intentional. It will not replace a design system or a layout tool, and that restraint is the point—when the need is “this UI, in a device, on a nice gradient,” the app is faster than opening a blank canvas elsewhere.

For DuckWare’s catalog it lands as a sharp open-source design utility: public app, forkable source, and extensions that meet creators where they already work.
