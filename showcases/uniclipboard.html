Cross-Device Sync

What is UniClipboard?

Universal clipboard tools either lock you into one vendor's ecosystem or route everything through a cloud service you have to trust. If you run Windows and Linux, want searchable history, or need to verify the encryption yourself, the options narrow fast.

UniClipboard syncs text, images, and files across desktops and mobile companions with no account and no central server reading your data. Devices join an encrypted "space" via invitation code and passphrase, connect peer-to-peer when possible, and fall back to a relay that only ever sees ciphertext. History is encrypted at rest with a searchable index that stays encrypted on disk.

Architectural Highlights & Strengths

  • P2P First, Encrypted Relay Fallback

    Desktop devices pair with a one-time public key exchange — no email signup. Direct connections work on the same Wi-Fi or across networks via NAT hole-punching; when that fails, an encrypted relay carries ciphertext only. Connections recover automatically after sleep, Wi-Fi switches, or brief disconnects.

  • XChaCha20-Poly1305 and Argon2id

    Payload encryption is independent of transport — a malicious relay cannot decrypt content. Keys derive from your passphrase via Argon2id, with the master key stored in the system keyring and per-space isolation so switching spaces re-encrypts local history under a new key.

  • Quick Panel, CLI, and Mobile Companion

    A keyboard-shortcut overlay previews text, links, images, and files without leaving your workflow. The uniclip CLI mirrors the GUI for headless servers and scripts. iOS and Android join as companions with QR pairing — share a phone screenshot to your desktop in seconds.

Getting Started

Create a space on your first device, then invite others with a short-lived code — or use the CLI for terminal workflows:

terminal
curl -fsSL https://uniclipboard.app/install.sh | bash

uniclip init
uniclip invite
uniclip join <code>
