DuckWare audit

A player that belongs on your desktop

Most music apps treat Linux as an afterthought — Electron shells, cloud logins, and themes that fight your wallpaper. omaTUNES flips that: it is a Rust-native Wayland client aimed at Hyprland and Omarchy rices, reading your library from disk and borrowing your system palette so the window feels like part of the compositor, not a guest.

Playback stays fully offline. Codecs arrive through Symphonia (MP3, FLAC, Opus, and friends), library state lives in plain JSON, and MPRIS2 plus a Waybar module keep transport controls in the bar while you work. Smart playlists, synced LRC lyrics, FFT visualizers, and bulk tag edits sit on top of that lean core — useful depth without a streaming stack.

It is a customized fork of sheep-farm/lavanda, shaped specifically for Omarchy’s opinionated look: theme presets (Nord, Catppuccin, Dracula, and more), WCAG-aware custom colors, and non-destructive browsing of folders you already keep. If your rice is the product, omaTUNES is the soundtrack that respects it.

Why it stands out

Three reasons to open it after login

Live desktop theming

Follow Omarchy’s system theme in real time, or lock a preset. Supporting shades derive automatically so accents stay readable on dark panels.

Local-only by design

No accounts, telemetry, or CDN hops. Likes, play counts, and playlists stay on your machine — one JSON file you can back up with everything else.

Compositor-native polish

MPRIS2, Waybar scripts, and a light Rust footprint fit Hyprland workflows. Manage large libraries without re-homing folders into a proprietary store.

Formats · Symphonia-native decode Playlists · rule-based smart sets Visualizer · nine FFT canvas modes Lyrics · synced LRC seek

Point it at ~/Music and press play

Pre-built binaries and a cargo release build are a few commands away. Pair it with the optional Waybar module when you want track info without focusing the window.
