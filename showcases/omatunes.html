omaTUNES

A native Wayland music player that lives in your local library and breathes your rice.

What is omaTUNES?

Most desktop music players were designed for GNOME or KDE and feel out of place on a tiling Wayland compositor. omaTUNES is a Rust-native player written for the Hyprland ecosystem — local files only, no streaming services, no account logins, no background sync daemons eating RAM.

It is a customized fork of lavanda by Balthazzahr, tuned specifically for Omarchy rices. Point it at your music folder and it handles playback with minimal chrome, leaving your workflow and window layout untouched.

Architectural Highlights & Strengths

  • Wayland-Native and Lightweight

    Built in Rust for direct Wayland rendering — no XWayland shim, no Electron shell. The player stays small on disk and in memory, which matters when you are running a compositor, terminal multiplexer, and a dozen other tools on the same machine.

  • Live Omarchy Theme Sync

    omaTUNES follows the active Omarchy theme automatically. Switch your rice and the player's accent colors update in real time — no manual palette editing or config file restarts required to keep the UI consistent with the rest of your desktop.

  • Offline-First Local Library

    Playback comes entirely from files on disk. There are no external API dependencies, no telemetry, and no network calls — just fast, predictable audio from a library you already own. Ideal for air-gapped setups or anyone who wants music playback without a cloud middleman.
